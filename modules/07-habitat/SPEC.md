## Overview

Cooper Station's interior must support 1 million inhabitants in an enclosed cylindrical environment with variable gravity. This module addresses urban planning, residential design, infrastructure, psychological considerations, and the unique architectural challenges posed by curved gravity and closed-system living.

```
                      = 80 km² floor area

Multi-story buildings (average 4 stories): 20 km² footprint required
```

### Building Architecture in Artificial Gravity

```
Curved gravity environment creates unique design needs:

1. RADIAL ORIENTATION (key difference from Earth):
   - Up/down always points toward/away from axis
   - Radial direction is functionally "vertical"
   - Horizontal (tangential) = Earth's horizontal

2. GRAVITY VECTOR CURVATURE:
   - Object dropped from 50m tall building
   - Lands vertically downward (toward rim)
   - Coriolis effect curves trajectory by ~5cm over drop
   - Building inhabitants adapt within days

3. ARCHITECTURAL IMPLICATIONS:
   - "Ground level" is always toward the rim (higher gravity)
   - "Sky" is always toward axis (lower gravity)
   - No north/south/east/west; use spinward/anti-spinward/inward/outward

4. BUILDING ORIENTATION:
   - Aligned radially (stacked toward rim)
   - Entrances at rim-side (lowest gravity entry)
   - Exits at spinward/anti-spinward edges
   - Basements extend outward (deeper gravity zones)

5. INTERNAL CIRCULATION:
   - Elevators run radially (normal gravity elevators)
   - Stairwells run radially (stairs become "slopes" if not aligned)
   - Corridors run axially (along cylinder length)
```

### Residential Building Design

```
Typical residential tower (64-story, 45m tall):

Lower levels (rim, 1.0g):
  Stores 1-20: Community spaces, shops, restaurants
  - Market-rate commercial rents subsidize housing
  - Public gathering spaces (crucial for community cohesion)

Middle levels (0.95-0.90g):
  Stores 21-50: Family apartments, two/three-bedroom units
  - Direct access to green spaces via internal gardens
  - Dedicated play areas for children

Upper levels (0.90-0.85g):
  Stores 51-64: Singles/couples studios and one-bedroom
  - Prime real estate (easier gravity environment)
  - Rooftop terraces with agriculture (urban farming)

Support systems (integrated into building):
  - Water risers (distribution & drainage)
  - Thermal piping (heating/cooling)
  - Fiber optic conduit (communications)
  - Pneumatic tube systems (mail/small package delivery)
  - Waste processing chutes (gravity-assisted)

Construction materials:
  - Steel frame (lightweight structures possible)
  - Regolith/fiberglass composite walls
  - Hydroponics on external walls (vertical farms)
  - Solar panels/radiators integrated into roof
```

## Psychological Considerations

### Enclosure Adaptation

```
Research findings from isolated research stations (Antarctica, submarines):
- 6-12 months: Acute adjustment period (highest psychological stress)
- 12-24 months: Moderate adaptation (depression rates elevated)
- 2-5 years: Most people adapt successfully
- 5+ years: Long-term stable, distinct micro-society develops

Mitigation strategies for Cooper Station:
1. VISUAL ILLUSION OF OPENNESS
   - Curved sky effect: Horizon always visible (psychological benefit)
   - Windows throughout (15-20% of surface strips dedicated)
   - "Sky" lighting cycles: Simulated day/night (25-hour cycle)

2. PSYCHOLOGICAL FREEDOM
   - No forced activities
   - Private spaces guaranteed (minimum 20 m² personal space)
   - Access to multiple recreation options
   - Choice in work/recreation/residence

3. COMMUNITY COHESION
   - Mandatory public gatherings (festivals, markets) for social bonding
   - Diverse multi-cultural population (prevents echo chambers)
   - Shared mission and identity (space pioneers)
   - Inter-district communication/travel easy

4. MENTAL HEALTH SERVICES
   - Mandatory initial counseling (all inhabitants)
   - Ongoing psychological monitoring (confidential)
   - Treatment for depression/anxiety easily accessible
   - Peer support groups organized by district

5. CONNECTION TO NATURE
   - Agricultural participation encouraged (everyone visits farms)
   - Wilderness parks with "natural" ecosystems
   - Pet ownership permitted (cats, dogs, aquariums)
   - Plant care in homes/workplaces
```

### Gravity Adaptation Physiology

```
Non-adapted residents (newly arrived):
- Symptoms: Vertigo, disorientation, clumsiness (first 48-72 hours)
- Severity: Moderate; well below space sickness threshold (0.5-2 RPM range)
- Duration: Adaptation typically complete within 2-7 days

Coriolis effect perception:
- Walking toward axis: Deflection feels like "leftward push" (spinward)
- Walking toward rim: Deflection feels like "rightward push"
- Throwing objects: Curves visible after ~1 second flight time
- Most people adapt unconsciously (no special training needed)

Long-term adaptation:
- Vestibular system recalibration: Takes 2-4 weeks
- Proprioceptive retraining: Unconscious (happens naturally)
- Behavioral adaptation: Coriolis incorporated into muscle memory within days

Children born in station:
- No adaptation period necessary
- Natural gravity reference frame from infancy
- Possible difficulty on Earth (reverse adaptation challenge)
```

### Diversity & Integration

```
Population composition targets:
- Multiple nations/cultures represented (avoid single-culture bias)
- Language diversity encouraged (at least 20 major languages)
- Cultural districts allowed but not enforced
- Intermarriage encouraged (genetic diversity benefit)
- Citizenship based on residence, not origin

Governance participation:
- Every adult has vote in quarterly district councils
- Proportional representation for major occupational groups
- Transparent decision-making (recorded and published)
- Term limits prevent power concentration
- Merit-based advancement in all professions
```
