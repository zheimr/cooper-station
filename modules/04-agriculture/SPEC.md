# Module 04: Agriculture & Food Production

**Status:** 🟡 In Progress
**Domain:** Agricultural Engineering, Botany, Nutrition Science

## Overview

The Agricultural module sustains a population of 1 million inhabitants through integrated food production systems operating across multiple gravity zones. This module encompasses hydroponic and aeroponic systems, conventional soil farming, livestock integration, and crop selection optimized for the enclosed environment and varying gravity fields.

## Agricultural Systems by Gravity Zone

### System 1: High-Intensity Hydroponics (0.75g - 1.0g, ~150 km²)

**Location:** Mid-level to rim zones

**Crops:** Leafy greens, herbs, fast-cycling vegetables
- Lettuce, kale, spinach, arugula
- Basil, parsley, cilantro
- Strawberries, tomatoes, peppers

**Production Metrics:**

```
Hydroponic crop yield: 8-10 kg/m²/year (vs. 2-4 kg/m²/year soil)
Area dedicated: 150 km² = 1.5 × 10¹¹ m²

Expected annual production:
  Low estimate: 8 kg/m² × 1.5 × 10¹¹ m² = 1.2 × 10¹² kg
  High estimate: 10 kg/m² × 1.5 × 10¹¹ m² = 1.5 × 10¹² kg

Caloric contribution (assuming 0.5 kcal/gram average):
  Low: 6.0 × 10¹¹ kcal/year
  High: 7.5 × 10¹¹ kcal/year — **Exceeds carbohydrate needs alone**
```

**Subsystems:**

1. **Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) channels**
   - Thin film of nutrient solution flows over plant roots
   - Water recovery: 95%+
   - Spacing: 30cm between plants, 2-3 crops/year rotation

2. **Vertical farming towers (10-15m tall)**
   - Stacked hydroponic beds utilizing gravity zones
   - LED grow lights (Module 05 power requirement: 200W per m² at peak)
   - Climate control integrated per zone

3. **Nutrient dosing & monitoring**
   - Precision nutrient tank (1,000 m³ central reservoir per 10 km²)
   - Real-time EC/pH monitoring (automated correction)
   - Nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium recycling from biosolids

4. **Water management**
   - Daily water input: ~50,000 m³ (much lower than soil farming)
   - Evapotranspiration loss: ~5-8%
   - Condensate recycling from ECLSS (Module 03) supplies 40% of need

---

### System 2: Aeroponic Production (0.5g - 0.75g, ~100 km²)

**Location:** Upper-mid to mid-level zones

**Crops:** Root vegetables, potatoes, beans, radish, turnips
- High-nutrient density staple crops
- Optimized for micro-gravity spray systems

**Production Metrics:**

```
Aeroponic yield: 6-9 kg/m²/year
Area: 100 km² = 1.0 × 10¹¹ m²

Expected annual production:
  Mid-range: 7.5 kg/m² × 1.0 × 10¹¹ m² = 7.5 × 10¹¹ kg
  Caloric contribution: 3.75 × 10¹¹ kcal/year (carbs + proteins)
```

**Subsystems:**

1. **Misting chamber design**
   - Ultrasonic nozzles create nutrient aerosol (10-100 μm droplets)
   - Spray interval: 10-15 seconds per cycle
   - Root chamber air circulation: 10-15 air changes/hour

2. **Gravity compensation**
   - Aerosol distribution must account for reduced gravity
   - Root support structure: biodegradable sponge or rockwool
   - Drainage to lower zones prevented by misting timing

3. **Oxygen injection**
   - Roots require dissolved O₂ in mist
   - Pressurized air line supplying 1-2 bar pressure
   - Air filtration (5 μm) to prevent microbial colonization

4. **Backup nitrogen fixation**
   - Integrated legume nodules for emergency N₂ fixation
   - Cyanobacteria bioreactors supplement ~5% of N requirement

---

### System 3: Soil Farming (Rim zone 0.9g-1.0g, ~150 km²)

**Location:** Rim agricultural strips (gravity most stable)

**Crops:** Grains, root crops, fiber plants, oil seeds
- Wheat, rice, barley (calorie staples)
- Potatoes, carrots, onions (nutritional diversity)
- Flax, hemp (fiber/oil)

**Production Metrics:**

```
Soil crop yield: 4-6 kg/m²/year (comparable to Earth agriculture)
Area: 150 km² = 1.5 × 10¹¹ m²

Expected annual production:
  Mid-range: 5 kg/m² × 1.5 × 10¹¹ m² = 7.5 × 10¹¹ kg
  Caloric contribution: 4.5 × 10¹¹ kcal/year
```

**Subsystems:**

1. **Engineered soil composition**
   - Base: Lunar regolith (chemically treated)
   - Amendment: Composted organic matter (2-3% by mass)
   - Texture optimization: 40% sand, 30% silt, 30% clay
   - Depth: 0.5-1.0m minimum for grain crops

2. **Irrigation system**
   - Drip irrigation using recycled greywater (Module 03)
   - Coverage: 100% of planted area
   - Daily consumption: ~200,000 m³ (allocated from ECLSS recovery)

3. **Pollination & genetic diversity**
   - Bee colonies (10,000 hives for 1M population)
   - Native plant species crossbreeding
   - Maintained seed bank (100 year supply) for genetic insurance

4. **Tillage & pest management**
   - Mechanical tilling synchronized with rotation cycle
   - Integrated pest management (no synthetic pesticides):
     - Beneficial insect populations
     - Crop rotation every 3-4 years
     - Quarantine zones for disease containment

## Livestock Integration

### Protein Production Systems

**Goal:** 20% of protein from animal sources (200,000 tonnes/year protein)

### System 1: Aquaculture (Tilapia, Salmon) - 0.5g zones

```
Fish density: 50 kg/m³ stocking
Water volume: 10 million m³ across distributed tanks
Annual production: 500,000 tonnes at 20% annual harvest

Caloric contribution: 2.5 × 10¹¹ kcal/year (protein-rich)
Feed conversion ratio: 1.8:1 (fish:feed)
Feed source: Agricultural byproducts + algae

Integration with ECLSS:
- Fish waste → biosolids recovery
- Ammonia scrubbing via nitrifying bacteria
- Integrated with Module 03 water processing
```

### System 2: Insect Farming (Crickets, Black Soldier Flies) - Low-g zones (0.25g-0.5g)

```
Advantages in low-gravity:
- More efficient protein conversion (2:1 feed ratio)
- Smaller environmental footprint
- Reduced water consumption

Production target: 200,000 tonnes/year insects
Facility footprint: 30 km² vertical stacking

Caloric contribution: 1.2 × 10¹¹ kcal/year
Byproducts: Chitin (industrial use), frass (fertilizer)
```

### System 3: Algae Protein (Spirulina, Chlorella) - All zones

```
High-efficiency protein production:
- Yield: 10-15 tonnes/hectare/year (vs. 0.5 tonnes for soy)
- Protein content: 60-70% dry weight
- Facility area: 20 km² bioreactors

Annual production target: 200,000 tonnes
Caloric contribution: 9.6 × 10¹¹ kcal/year (potential)

Integration:
- Photobioreactors in low-g zones
- Powered by LED arrays (Module 05)
- CO₂ from ECLSS integration (Module 03)
```

### Mammalian Livestock (Limited)

```
Space constraints limit to:
- Dairy cattle: 5,000 head (milk, cheese, yogurt)
- Poultry: 500,000 birds (eggs, meat)
- No beef/pork (inefficient feed conversion in limited space)

Housed in 0.75g-0.95g zones with climate control
Waste → biosolids processing (Module 03)
Annual production: 50,000 tonnes animal products
```

## Crop Rotation & Yield Scheduling

### Three-Rotation Cycle (4-month cycles)

```
Cycle 1 (Months 1-4):   Leafy greens + legumes + root crops
Cycle 2 (Months 5-8):   Brassicas + alliums + fruiting crops
Cycle 3 (Months 9-12):  Tubers + grains + fiber crops

Staggered across zones ensures continuous harvest
Legume integration fixes nitrogen (reduces fertilizer need)
```

### Peak vs. Storage Requirement

```
Peak production: 2.4 × 10⁹ kcal/day
Average consumption: 2.0 × 10⁹ kcal/day

Seasonal variation buffer: ±15%
Storage capacity required: 2 months × 2.0 × 10⁹ kcal/day
Frozen/preserved storage: 1.2 × 10¹¹ kcal

Cold storage zones (0-5°C): 50,000 m³
Dry storage (ambient): 100,000 m³
Fermentation storage: 20,000 m³
```

## Mass & Nutrient Cycling

### Nitrogen Cycle

```
Sources:
  Legume nitrogen fixation: 200 tonnes N/year
  Algae/cyanobacteria: 100 tonnes N/year
  Mined from regolith: 50 tonnes N/year

Sinks:
  Crop uptake: 300 tonnes N/year
  Livestock feed: 50 tonnes N/year

Closure: Composting returns 95% of N to soil
Target: <5% external N input after steady-state reached
```

### Phosphorus & Potassium Cycling

```
Aquaculture provides bio-available phosphorus
Fish waste → biosolids → agricultural amendment

Potassium sourced from:
- Regolith mineral processing
- Biosolids recycling
- Mineral-rich dust from impact geology

Tight cycle achieved through composting + biosolids recovery
```
